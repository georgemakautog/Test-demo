What is a Motherboard?

A motherboard is the main circuit board that connects all the essential components of a computer, including the CPU, GPU, RAM, storage devices, and peripheral interfaces. It provides the pathways for data communication and power distribution across the system.

Motherboards come in various form factors such as ATX, Micro-ATX, and Mini-ITX, each offering different levels of expandability and features. High-end motherboards support advanced overclocking, multiple GPU setups, and enhanced cooling solutions.

Choosing the right motherboard ensures system stability, compatibility, and future-proofing for upgrades. It is truly the backbone of any computer, enabling all components to work seamlessly together for both performance and reliability.
