What is a CPU?

A CPU, or Central Processing Unit, is the brain of the computer. It performs instructions from programs, executes calculations, and manages data flow between memory, storage, and other components.

CPUs are essential for general computing tasks, running applications, and enabling multitasking. They vary in cores, threads, and clock speeds, directly affecting the speed and efficiency of your computer.

Modern CPUs support advanced technologies like multithreading, virtualization, and energy efficiency, making them suitable for gaming, professional work, software development, and scientific computations.
